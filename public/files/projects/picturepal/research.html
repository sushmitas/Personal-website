01 Research Overview

In order to find out what really matters, we did over fifteen interviews with people who currently have roommates. In the process of our investigation we found a number of needs that seemed important to our target user group. Here are a few:

  • Cleaning: Messes were a topic of contention among roommates.
  • Rules: Many households did not have set rules, but rather operated using the reasonable person principle. (Ie. Most people are reasonable so if you ask them nicely, they will try to change their ways.) This could potentially, lead to problems.
  • Social: One of the things that people really enjoyed about having roommates was just having someone around.
  • Privacy: Sometimes people just want to be left alone sometimes.
  • Money: Coordinating payments (ie. Rent, utilities etc.) is always a chore.
  • Noise: Complaints about the volume level seemed to be prevalent.

There are situations where a person may be doing something that is annoying and his/her roommate but the roommate does not want to raise issue with it so it goes unaddressed while slowly nagging the roommate. Initially, we wanted to create an agent that would mediate these socially awkward situations.
