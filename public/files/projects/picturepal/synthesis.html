01 Synthesis Overview

After our initial directed storytelling interviews the feedback we received is that people would not want to buy a product to help avert social awkwardness because they might not admit such a problem existed (no perceived need). Roommates in our study did not focus on the negative aspects. Instead, we found a large amount of focus placed on the positive aspects of having a roommate and the social benefits this provides.

With this in mind, we did ideation focusing on the positive parts of living together. We came up with 64 ideas for smart home devices. We then proceeded to create an affinity diagram grouping the ideas into different categories to come up with the underlying needs and see which needs were recurring and seemed to be the most valuable. This process led to a set of 6 needs which we then turned into storyboard sketches for concept validation. At concept validation, we took 14 sketches and met with 11 roommates to find out which of our ideas would resonate with them and what needs would turn out to be important. At the conclusion of our concept validation we had two strong contenders and it was a very tough decision of which one we should choose. In the end, we chose Kodak Moments/PicturePal because it emphasized the social relationship between roommates which seemed to be important to them, even more so than helping them deal with issues that exist when people live together.
