05 Validation Results

Roommate Set 1

need: cooordination device
Not necessarily wishlist - but *centralized shopping list* for the apartment. this would be useful.
ppl buy things with the intent of sharing with roommates

need: favors
favors just happen, you can't control them. they wouldn't want structure in place for this.
Makes extra food on purpose, sometimes to share, sometimes for the next day. offer it if other sare around the kitchen, but never planned. no desire to coordinate dinner schedules and dietary restrictions.

need: appreciation
-not as important of a need
would probably just thank them the next time they saw them if they weren't around. when someone's doing a chore, someone else is usually around to pitch in.
might not take a reward system seriously. structured system would fade over time. incentives would fade over time.
the trigger to clean is when the mess is restricting from doing something.
when someone goes out of their way, it would be nice to get appreciation, but in a spontaneous way. shouldn't be required to give thanks.

need: awareness
they're pretty good right now about being aware. good at communicating already when in person.
this would be really useful to find out how others are feeling when you're not around each other.
liked the noiseminder - ppl often have to remind each other to put the volume down.
it would be useful to see *who's home* at what time - helps coordinate social activities. otherwise hard to coordinate with so many ppl.

need: communication/coordination
they talk a lot so no need for technology to help with communication, but would be helpful for coordination
doorchat would be cool - maybe it should be in a central place for asynchronous communication? usually if they're not working, they'd be downstairs together. don't mind it if ppl interrupt them and come into their room.
*showerviz* - would be useful. in their house ppl have their own showers, but with common space it would be useful.
*kitchenviz* would definitely be useful b/c there's not a ton of counter space.
TV viz - mabye useful depending on the ppl
they want this to be like a reservation system - let system know in advance

need: social
ppl def have great house moments. *kodak moments* could be fun. right now ppl just email photos out. came up with picture frame where you could toggle thru photos in the house and visitors could see activities of the house.
specific need: not only capturing the photos, but *sharing* the photos with ppl outside of the house. showing off how good their social relationship is
house robot - not that useful. they have a common shelf where they leave stuff so ppl know others can take it. robots can be novelty/fun but not useful.
comic book - interesting idea. could help invite ppl to events (awareness) and works as a common calendar (coordination).
specific need: communication/coordination

need: responsibility
location stick - ppl know where the mess is, but someone putting the stick down anonymously shows they're concerned about a mess. this works as an opinion system. not obnoxious but still shows that something annoys you.
specific need: addresses social awkwardness
Kill the Dirt Pet - would really annoy b/c cleaning is not fun and the pet would make it worse.
Collect Em All - gold star system didn't work when you were a kid either.

 

Roommate Set 2
*idea name here* = they liked it

Favors
wishlist: One gives the other rides to costco and has also sometimes just bought mt. dew (which other roommate loves) for him without being asked.
dinnerbell: They always make extra food. They didn't feel a need for this. Pretty much if someone was around then they would give them the extra and if not it would be packed up and put in the fridge. They could see if each other are home and proceed appropriately.

Didn't like the appreciation solutions:
Leaving a note for another person thanking them interferes with the spontaneousness of it and takes away genuineness.
People do the dishes and no one ever knows who did it. Still didn't feel the need for this solution.
kudos didn't like because he felt that if making reward extrinsic takes away from intrinsic motivation. Will end up doing it less or at least feeling like doing chores less.

Magnettug- didn't like because generally when they are in bad moods they want the option to seal themselves off from the world. Don't want sympathy (this may be a male vs. female thing or extrovert vs. introvert thing.) If they want other people to know how they are feeling they use the away messages in Aim/gmail etc.
Roomlight- privacy issues. also same as above.
*Noiseminder*- they both liked and thought it was useful. After being probed, they said that they thought it was helpful both from the side when they want to go to sleep but also when they are making the noise. Their perception is that people are never noisy on purpose just don't realize they are waking other ppl up.
*Doorchat*- They currently use im/e-mail for this. They don't feel like the need it to show they are busy/studying but may be more helpful for leaving notes for later such as that someone called for them. They both liked it and thought it was useful.
showerviz- this is a problem. but after admitting it was a problem both of them basically said it isn't really. other roommate has no pattern. user takes at night but this could be any time between 6pm-6am (whenever he goes to sleep). Kitchenviz- not a problem. One uses stove, one uses oven.

They feel in general they are good about communication (when they are pissed off), and don't need help facilitating it.

Kodakmoment- privacy issues. visual not helpful as far as they are concerned. Would be much more interested in a quote keeper that recorded funny lines or quotes. They thought all the pictures would end up looking the same and no one would have any idea what they were of. Also a problem managing/organizing them.

Robot- they were amused by it and liked it but it didn't seem like they really needed it.
househeroes- ditto.

*locationsticks*- "We would have the best catologued mess."
Seemed to like it although, I am not sure from what they said if they would really use it or just throw a stick in a pile and then be like, oh I tagged it. Seemed to think it was a good idea though. reminder to do cleaning, favors, responsiblility (3 of our themes) all together. Also, could be used as a gps tagging type of things. Want to remember where something is, throw a stick at it. Want to remember to take something out with you, could throw a stick at it . Could be used in more than one way.

kill the dirt pet- "don't need to make mess personified" would not find motivating.
collectemall- want to be able to choose when to do favors for friends, not be forced into it.

 

Roommates Set 3 and 4

*wishlist*: good way to coordinate buying groceries. right now, roommateJ just buys stuff for himsefl b/c they have no concept of what roommateG would need. would be helpful to coordinate.
roommateE thinks it makes sense, but she shops with her roommate, and with her old roommate they had a standard list of shared groceries. and whoever was at the groceries would buy it.
roommateA thinks it would be useful to know what roommateE needs. roommateE prob wouldnt make such a list, but roommateA would be useful with other roommates.
shared shopping list . maybe if it knows where you are it could give contextual reminders.
maybe could tie in with moodboard: "your roommate is grumpy and they like ice cream when they're grumpy."

dinnerbell: roommateJ cooks and sometimes there's extra and tell the guys that they can eat. happens seldom that they are all there at the same time.
if roommateE and roommateA are cooking extra food it is to share. usually they arrange schedule to cook together. lots of planning to coordinate dinners together. go to groceries together. more of a need for coordination system.

thankyou note: set3 liked the idea of having a noninvasive way of thanking someone.
set4 think that the reward is diff than thank you notes. roommateJ would clean the bathroom if roommateG cleaned the kitchen.

ppl seem to have roles that have come about, but not assigned. incremental cleaning. also usually spontaneous cleaning when there's a huge mess or when a guest comes.

kudos: would have to be electronic and automatically tallied.
appreciation is more implicit in the things ppl do for each other. generally supposed to do half of it.
ppl spend a lot of time cleaning and other person doesnt always notice. when set4 do work it's always visible.
*would be nice to have an easy non-intrusive way to make it apparent that someone did a lot of work and thank the other person.
roommateG worried about token economy thing - if there is an imbalance in that, it would be tangible and might increase tension.

sometimes systems in place for this appreciation or chore dividing indicate that there is something wrong. the ppl end up interacting more formally.

magnettug: set3 would want each other to know each other's mood. would need more than happy/sad - more like "don't bug me"
roommateJ thinks it would be really good - household, you have a closer relationship. if you don't know each other's moods, it can be an awkward situation.

both agreed that communication is important. "happy/sad' not indicative - should indicate what they expect their roommate to do "
"i'm in a bad mood, f* off" vs. "i'm in a bad mood, and i want you to comfort me"

roomlight: want to be able to shut it off. the color thing could be really need - has a big impact
if the room is dirty, you'd like the lights to dim so you don't have to see it. could also show roommate you think it's messy.

noiseminder: roommateG wishes he had this as an undergrad. roommateJ thinks it's really useful - studying w/ loud music and forgets the time.
good way to save face without having to tell them to turn it down. even neighbors could tie this in.
set3 think it should detect how loud the sound would seem to the other person.

doorchat: kinda what IM is for already. roommateA cant see her bedroom door from where she's sitting. they already IM each other from their rooms.
roommateJ thinks it would make sense to have it in a central place for asynchronous messages. more like a central whiteboard/blackboard. works well when you're totally on diff. schedules. digital version that could connect to your mobile devices. could send and get stuff from it from remote locations.

*showerviz*: seems useful if you have a lot of roommates. coordinating with one isnt hard. with roommateE's old roommate, it would be useful.
roommateJ thinks it would be useful b/c it's a single resource and has potential for conflicts. sometimes they all get up at the same time.
would work well as a reservation system - maybe could tap into schedule to estimate when ppl are likely to use it.
erin thinks it would def work well for TV.
make showertime more efficient, valuable - allow ppl to see presentations, etc.
could even coordinate this for when they're going grocery shopping and when they're out of stuff.

*kodakmoments*: could be really fun. should have an off the record mode. should be able to annotate the pics.

houserobot: why couldnt ppl just present it yourself? should do the crapwork.
in terms of sharing stuff, it's more of a "come and get some" - kitchen counter and stove.

*househeroes*: roommateG likes comic books, but would like it to not be about chores. likes the celebration thing.
would be nice to reflect on last week's to see what they didnt do. could almost work in conjunction with the pictures.
reflecting backwards would be a lot better than reflecting forwards.

*location stick*: roommateE would rather just say she's annoyed about it. roommateA thinks it could be cool instead of saying you're annoyed. maybe there should be some text along with it. marking the door might be ambiguous - so text or addiitonal info would help. roommateG kinda likes the idea of throwing something to mark it.
could also work as a reminder for yourself as well. get a sense of checking them off when you finish it.
could also work as an indicator to show your roommates you're gonna do it and you're aware of the mess.
location stick could change color if you havent done it - as a 2nd reminder. (have a timer built in)

kill the dirt pet: lukewarm

collect them all: roommateG says if roommateJ alwyas wanted to clean, then he'd gladly take him out
should have them work together for a shared reward. system could tell them they're really close to their goal. dont know if there's much incentive to have their room compete against another room. would work if it wasnt so visible and explicit to everyone. the system should take on the chore of doing difficult stuff of reminding ppl they need to do stuff. instead of everyone seeing that one person isnt doing a lot of stuf in the house, it could show them that another person is doing a lot and they should pick up the slack.
