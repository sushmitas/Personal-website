01 Solution Overview

After receiving feedback on our rough video sketch we went back and reshot some of the visuals, rerecorded some lines from the audio, changed the background music and changed the timing and transitions. Basically, our motto for a couple of days was "edit! edit! edit!". We gave a final presentation on May 9th, 2007 in which we delivered a powerpoint presentation and played our video demo for a room full of people. Thankfully, it was really well received!

Looking back, one of the interesting things about this project is how the focus shifted from beginning to end. We were looking for a problem to fix. We were trying to find where communication and coordination between roommates breaks down. But roommates reported that they didn't have any problems that they weren't able to solve easily. Discussing this with them elicited the need for house pride, showing others that they were good roommates, that they got along well. Ultimately, the lack of perceived problems drew us away from the problem space and towards the social space instead. Thus, our final design uses house pride and social bonding to enhance the feeling of closeness between roommates rather than addressing the problems that they may have. And we learned that sometimes the problem you are looking for is not necessarily the problem that should be "fixed".
